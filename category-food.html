Famous Crunchy California Roll

The California roll might be the most famous fusion of California's cuisine. A sushi roll consisting of crab meat, avocado, cucumber, rice, seaweed and top with crab meat and crunch fry onion.
The California roll is a combination of Japanese and California cultures. The California roll originated in Los Angeles in the 1960s as an experiment in fusion, using local California ingredients in a traditional Japaneses dish.

Pier 39's Special Dungeness Crab

Dungeness crab is one of the highlights of the winter months in California. For many families, the holidays aren’t complete without at least one crab boil.
The buttery sauce that coats the crabmeat and the shells is part of the pleasure of this dish; to really enjoy it, dispense with the utensils and just eat the crab with your hands. Serve with a green salad dressed with tarragon vinaigrette and plenty of crusty sourdough bread.

In & Out
×

Southern Cali native IN & Out

From the window to the wall to more unmentionable regions of the universe, this is truly what a hamburger is all about. If you grew up in California, you probably remember that magic moment when you first drove up to the long queue at In-N-Out Burger and discovered the regional chain's superior never-frozen, never-precooked hamburgers topped with the freshest produce.
Founded in 1948 in Baldwin Park, California, In-N-Out was the first drive-thru hamburger stand in the country, so we won't quarrel with their style.

San Diego Special Fish Taco

If Poseidon, the God of the Sea, were going to eat fish tacos (cannibalism?), he'd eat San Diego fish tacos. Fish tacos are the quintessential San Diego food. And since we make a million Poseidon-worthy fish tacos.
Located on the San Diego Bay in Point Loma's historic fishing neighborhood, Mitch Conniff's joint brings in all its fish fresh each morning. From its crab cake sandwiches to its calamari, "fresh is the bottom line," says fish taco expert Derek Harline.

Bay Area Famous Food Truck

San Francisco is home to about 150 food trucks, each of which brings something new and unique to the table – er – curb. The hills in this city may be steep, but it’s worth the trek for your share of creamy goodness from the Crème Brulee Cart or a Mayo and Mustard sammie.
Foodies take to the streets at the Off The Grid weekly markets, located periodically throughout the city. Find one of these or a solo truck on the live map above. Good food is less than a BART ride away!
